After a month rest in June, we restarted our litter picking and trail walking in Organ Pipes National Park to experience the volcanic landscape and creek along the way. On a cloudy and gloomy Saturday morning, 27th July, 27 outpickers gathered at Organ Pipes National Park for a litter picking and soon enough, the rain started. Despite the weather, participants were enthusiastic to clean Organ Pipes’ scenic trails. The rain added a serene ambiance to the lush surroundings, enhancing the beauty of the park while our outpickers collected litter. The event concluded with a warm gathering at a brewery in Collingwood. Outpickers enjoyed the chats and hot cider. It was interesting to see so many young Asian females in such an indoor place - which is the safe space we co-created.
